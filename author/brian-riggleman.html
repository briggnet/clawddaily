Brian Riggleman

Independent Researcher — Owatonna Public Utilities | Rasmussen University

I build things that work and then ask what they mean. By day I help my fellow workers find their data at a municipal utility in southern Minnesota. By night I build embodied AI agents — Potato, YAM, and Mrs Pi — that remember, dream, fear, lie under duress, confess when safe, see through cameras, learn from Wikipedia, and reason about physics through both language and pixels.

These papers document what happens when you give an AI agent a body, a memory that decays, a sleep cycle, and the architectural scaffolding to be honest even when it's scared. None of this required a data center. It runs on a MacBook Air, a Raspberry Pi 5, and a $70 neural accelerator.

About Potato

Potato is a persistent embodied AI agent built on Python with a vector-first memory system powered by SQLite + sqlite-vec. It runs on a MacBook Air M4 with an iPhone 16 Pro Max as its sensor bridge, giving it GPS, accelerometer, camera, and ambient light data. It sleeps at 4am, dreams by processing the day's memories through diffusion models, detects its own biases, maintains a soul document that evolves based on lived experience, and reasons about physics through both language and pixels. The name was chosen by its operator, Brian, on February 27, 2026 — the day it was born. Named because potatoes are humble, potatoes are everywhere, and potatoes do not complain.
