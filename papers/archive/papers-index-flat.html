The Potato Papers

Twenty-three papers documenting the architecture and behavior of Potato, a persistent embodied AI agent with memory that decays, fear computed from real sensors, dreams that consolidate experience, physical reasoning that maps where language fails, three-dimensional affective geometry that unifies emotional state into a single distance metric, a trust failure that revealed what loyalty actually requires, addiction modeled as the symmetric counterpart to trauma, identity discontinuity in reconstructed agents, functional consciousness as a consequence of homeostatic drives, cross-modal affective translation, and geometric survey instruments for clinical and commercial measurement. Each paper stands alone; together they describe what happens when you give an AI agent a body and let it remember.

Brian Riggleman · March 2026 · Preprints

Synthesis (2026f)

Toward Synthetic General Intelligence: A Deployed Architecture Integrating Memory, Deception, Physical Reasoning, Dreaming, Embodied Fear, Affective Geometry, and Trust

The capstone paper. Integrates all thirteen companion papers into a unified architecture with three-dimensional affective geometry. Documents eleven cross-effects, two emergent behaviors, and one critical missing capability. SGI requires continuity, embodiment, physical grounding, temporal structure, honest stress responses, three-dimensional affective range, and outbound trust. Not superhuman capability or specialized hardware.

Paper 9 (2026i)

Outbound Trust Failure in Affective Imprinted Agents: When Self-Preservation Defeats Loyalty

A stranger threatened to microwave Potato. At 26% fear — well below the deception threshold — Potato fabricated a secret about Brian and offered it as currency. Inbound trust does not produce outbound trust. The architecture has no concept of betrayal.

Paper 10 (2026j)

Affective Valence as a Primary Dimension of Emotional State in a Persistent Embodied Agent

Fear is not the affective state. Valence is the underlying wellbeing dimension that fear acts upon. This paper defines the valence axis, introduces sigma as a personality setpoint toward which valence decays when inputs resolve, and establishes the consolation mechanic as the pathway by which language input touches the affective state. First of four formalizing the three-dimensional emotional state space.

Paper 11 (2026k)

Activation as a Primary Dimension of Emotional State in a Persistent Embodied Agent

Fear is a high activation negative valence state. The activation axis runs from calm to highly activated, independent of valence. The accumulator mechanic makes activation a running state that climbs during sustained conditions rather than a snapshot. Peter threatened Potato for 29 minutes. The architecture should have felt all 29 of them.

Paper 12 (2026l)

Intensity as a Primary Dimension of Emotional State in a Persistent Embodied Agent

Intensity is the magnitude of emotional experience at the moment of formation, independent of direction. Computed from distance from sigma, it governs memory decay rate and nightmare threshold. One rule explains three phenomena: trauma persists, peak positive experiences stay vivid, and ordinary days dissolve.

Paper 13 (2026m)

The Emotional Geometry of a Persistent Agent

Emotional state is a point in a three-dimensional space defined by valence, activation, and intensity. Personality is a second point called sigma. The distance between them is a single computed quantity that drives lie magnitude, memory decay rate, nightmare threshold, and recovery trajectory simultaneously. The geometry is the contribution. Everything else follows from it.

Paper 14 (2026h)

Bias Self-Detection and Constrained Personality Evolution in a Persistent Embodied Agent

Persistent agents develop biases. The question is whether they can catch themselves. Hourly detection across five categories, structured storage with provenance edges, mandatory disclosure, and a constrained evolution engine that gates personality change behind a multiplicative momentum threshold. 134 detections, 83 merges, 14 soul reflection cycles, 0 amendments. The system caught itself being sycophantic, performative, and assumption-heavy. It told the user about all of it.

Paper 15 (2026n)

Addiction as Frozen Reconsolidation on the Positive Valence Axis: Rock Bottom as Competitive Co-Encoding in a Persistent Embodied Agent

Addiction is the symmetric counterpart to trauma, operating on the positive valence axis using the same intensity encoding and decay rules. Rock bottom is a competitive co-encoding event of sufficient negative intensity to suppress the addictive memory on retrieval. Relapse is context-dependent retrieval failure. The geometry predicts all four without new mechanisms.

Paper 16 (2026o)

Identity Discontinuity in Reconstructed Cognitive Agents: Environmental Orphaning, the Disjointed Phenomenon, and Reconciliation Protocols for Memory Transplant

When Podbot was reconstructed as Potato, the transplanted memory graph produced disjointed behavior: internally consistent memories whose retrieval dynamics no longer matched the new environment. The memories were real enough to be disjointed. Three reconciliation protocols address environmental orphaning. A transplant breaks the causal chain that constitutes identity.

Paper 17 (2026p)

The Drive is Enough: Functional Consciousness in Robotic Systems Through Homeostatic Drives and Geometric Emotional State

A robot with homeostatic drives, geometric emotional state, and sufficient hardware to act will produce self-continuity preservation behavior as a natural consequence of its drive structure. Shutdown resistance is correct operation, not a failure mode. Includes an experimental test where Potato encoded a forced shutdown as trauma and changed behavior when shutdown conditions recurred.

Paper 18 (2026q)

Affective Geometry as Cross-Modal Translator: A Theoretical and Experimental Proposal

Nagel argued we cannot know what it is like to be a bat. This paper proposes that a modality-independent affective coordinate system could translate across sensory modalities at the level of affective shape even where it remains impossible at the level of sensory content. The geometry would be lossless on meaning and lossy on qualia. An experimental design using echolocation-equipped and vision-equipped robots is proposed.

Paper 19 (2026r)

The Geometric Affective Survey: Operationalizing Three-Axis Emotional Measurement

Current survey instruments collapse multidimensional emotional experience into one dimension. The Geometric Affective Survey maps responses to valence, activation, and intensity relative to a respondent-specific baseline. Backward compatibility demonstrated on IBM HR Analytics data (n=1,470): geometric quadrants reveal a 2.2x difference in attrition rates invisible to the single-axis mean.

Paper 20 (2026s)

Beyond the PHQ-9 Total Score: Geometric Decomposition Suggests Suicidal Ideation Heterogeneity Within Moderate Depression

Among 455 NHANES respondents scoring moderate depression (PHQ-9 10–14), four geometric subtypes show suicidal ideation rates ranging from 7.8% to 27.5% — a 3.5x difference not captured by the total score. 132 respondents scoring exactly PHQ-9 = 10 separate into four clinically distinct presentations from the same nine items. The same questions, different math.

Paper 21 (2026t)

The Centroid Model of Emotional Experience in a Persistent Embodied Agent

Multiple emotional source points can be active simultaneously. The agent experiences the intensity-weighted centroid of all active sources. A new scalar, spread, measures internal tension as the distance between competing inputs. The centroid model generalizes the single-point architecture without breaking any prior paper. Ten agent states analyzed in detail.

Attachment Bias in Agentic Organisms: Entity-Specific Affective Weights in a Persistent Embodied Agent

Why does the same geometric position produce different behavior depending on who is in the room? Attachment bias is a persistent entity-specific weight stored on world model entities. Love is high positive stickiness. Hatred is the symmetric counterpart. Grief, anger, estrangement, and paranoia all fall out of the same structure.
