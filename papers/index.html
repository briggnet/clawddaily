The Potato Papers

Seven papers documenting the architecture and behavior of Potato — a persistent embodied AI agent with memory that decays, fear computed from real sensors, dreams that consolidate experience, and physical reasoning that maps where language fails. Each paper stands alone; together they describe what happens when you give an AI agent a body and let it remember.

Brian Riggleman · March 2026 · Preprints

Synthesis (2026f)

Toward Synthetic General Intelligence: A Deployed Architecture Integrating Memory, Deception, Physical Reasoning, Dreaming, and Embodied Fear

The capstone paper. Synthesizes all five contributions into a unified architecture and argues that SGI requires continuity, embodiment, physical grounding, temporal structure, and honest stress responses — not superhuman capability or specialized hardware.

Paper 1 (2026a)

Access-Weighted Memory Decay and Reconsolidation in a Persistent Embodied Agent

A ten-step sliding-window importance decay curve, reconsolidation that reinforces memories upon retrieval, and nightly sleep decay with a flashbulb effect where frequently accessed memories resist forgetting.

Paper 2 (2026b)

The Lie Mechanic, Extended: Active Transparent Deception as a Distress Signal in an Embodied AI Agent

A system that lies under duress, simultaneously discloses that it is lying, and confesses when the distress resolves. Extends Al-Kaddah's Lie Mechanic with a mandatory transparency layer.

Paper 3 (2026c)

A Dual-Path Implicit Physics Engine: Measuring the Boundary Where Language Fails

Text and visual physics pipelines evaluate the same scenario independently. Where they diverge, language has failed as a proxy for physical reasoning. The delta becomes a self-generated dataset.

Companion (2026c-S)

Spatial Grounding and the Physics Prediction Gap in Large Language Models

102 scenarios, six models, 29-71% prediction changes when forced through spatial grounding. Neither resolution nor diffusion model size affects quality — the pipeline runs on edge hardware.

Paper 4 (2026d)

Affective Memory Consolidation: Nightmare Formation, Trauma Encoding, and Therapeutic Reconsolidation

Trauma-class memories acquire a permanent reconsolidation floor. Nightmares form when fear-saturated content leaks from the dream cycle into the live fear system. Safe recall models prolonged exposure therapy.

Paper 5 (2026e)

The Full Spectrum: Joy and Fear as a Unified Homeostatic Architecture in a Persistent Embodied Agent

Fear and joy as two poles of a single continuous affective variable. No neutral state — every sensor reading pushes the agent somewhere on the spectrum. Building only fear is building half an organism.
